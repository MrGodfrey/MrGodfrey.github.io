About me

Welcome to my personal webpage. I am an Assistant Professor of Mathematics at Southwest Jiaotong University.

My research primarily focuses on control theory for stochastic partial differential equations (SPDEs) and inverse problems within this context. These areas of mathematics are crucial for understanding systems affected by random disturbances, which have significant implications in various scientific and engineering applications.

One of my key interests is the controllability of SPDEs. Controllability pertains to the capacity to guide a system from any initial state to a desired final state within a finite time, using appropriate controls. The randomness intrinsic to SPDEs makes this a complex challenge, as many tools and methods for deterministic systems become ineffective.

My work in this field focuses on null controllability for linear fourth-order SPDEs. By utilizing duality arguments, I have simplified null controllability to the observability of backward fourth-order SPDEs. I established the essential observability estimate through the development of a global Carleman estimate.

I also devote attention to semi-discrete systems as they serve as approximations of continuous systems. In this context, I have investigated the null controllability of some semi-discrete SPDEs, introducing new, refined semi-discrete Carleman estimates.

Another focal point of my research is inverse problems in SPDEs. Broadly, inverse problems involve determining a system's underlying parameters or causes based on observed outcomes, given a mathematical model of the system.

In this realm, I have explored ill-posed Cauchy problems involving stochastic parabolic equations. My contributions include deriving the conditional stability and convergence rate of the Tikhonov regularization method for these ill-posed Cauchy problems.
