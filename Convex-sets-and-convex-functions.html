Convex sets and convex functions

2019-01-19 | 数学 ， 学习笔记

The notes about convex sets and convex funcitons.

Let \(\Omega\) be a convex open set in \(\mathbb R^n\).

Definition. A function \(f: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb R\) is called convex provided \[ f(\tau x + (1-\tau)y) \leq \tau f(x)+(1-\tau)f(y), \] holds for all \(x,y\in \Omega\) and each \(0\leq \tau \leq 1\).

Theorem 1. A function \(f\) is convex if and only if its epigraph \(\Lambda_f\) is a convex set, where \(\Lambda_f :=\{ (x,y) |x\in \Omega, f(x)\leq y \} \subset \mathbb{R}^n\).

Proof. This is a direct inference of definition.

Theorem 2. A function \(f\) is convex, if and only if for all \(x\in \Omega\), there exists a non-zero vector \(r=r(x) \in \mathbb R^n\) such that \[ f(y)\geq f(x)+ r\cdot(y-x) \] holds for all \(y \in \Omega\).

Proof. Firstly, we proof necessity. Thanks to theorem 1, epigraph \(\Lambda_f\) is convex. For all \(x \in \Omega\), \((x,f(x))\) is at the boundary of \(\Lambda_f\). According to the supporting hyperplane theorem for convex set, there exists \(a=a(x) \in \mathbb R^{n+1}\) not equals to zero, such that \[ a \cdot (x^*-x, y^*-f(x)) \geq 0 \] holds for all \((x^*,y^*)\in \Lambda_f\), in particular for \((y,f(y))\). Denote \(a=(a_1,\cdots,a_{n+1})\), then we have \(a_{n+1}\neq 0\).

In fact, assuming \(a_{n+1}=0\), then \((a_1,\cdots, a_n) \cdot (y-x)\geq 0\) holds for all \(y\in \Omega\), in particularly for \(y \in B_r(x) \subset \Omega\), where \(B_r(x)\) is a ball centered at \(x\), thanks to the fact that \(\Omega​\) is an open set. That leads to the contradictory.

Without loss of generality, we can assume \(a_{n+1} > 0\), and let \(a=a_{n+1}(-r,1)\). Then \[ -r(y-x)+f(y)-f(x)\geq 0 \] holds for all \(y \in \Omega\) which completes the necessity.

As for sufficiently, fix $x_1, x_2 $ and \(\tau \in (0,1)\). Let \(x^*=\tau x_1 + (1-\tau)x_2\), \(y^*=\tau f(x_1) + (1-\tau)f(x_2)\). It follows from the assumption that \[ (-r,1) \cdot (y-x^*,f(y)-f(x^*)) \geq 0 ,\forall y \in \Omega. \] According to the definition of \(\Lambda_f\), we have \[ (-r,1) \cdot (x-x^*,y-f(x^*)) \geq 0 ,\forall (x,y) \in \Lambda_f. \] Substituting \((x,y)\) by \((\tau x_1, \tau f(x_1))\) and \(((1-\tau)x_2, (1-\tau )f(x_2))\) implies that \((-r,1) \cdot (0,y^*-f(x^*)) \geq 0\), which shows \(y^*\geq f(x^*)\). It follows that the epigraph \(\Lambda_f\) is convex, which completes the proof thanks to theorem 1.

Remark. The function \(f\) is convex if and only if \(\Gamma^+=\Omega\).
