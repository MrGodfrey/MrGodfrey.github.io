How does the second order EPDE equation change after the variables are transformed

2018-05-31 | 2018-11-13 | 数学 ， 小问题

When I was reading Gilberd's Elliptic partial differential equation of second order chapter 6, I found this technique very useful. How does the second order EPDE equation change after the variables are transformed? It can be used to simplify equation.

question

In the equation \[ A^{ij} D_{ij} u=f(x), \quad A^T=A, \] let \(\{A^{ij}\}\) be a constant matrix.

Let \(P\) be a constant matrix which defines a nonsingular linear transformation \(y=xP\) from \(\mathbb{R}^n\) onto \(\mathbb{R}^n\). Letting \(u(x) \rightarrow \tilde{u}(y)\) under this transformation, one verifies easily that \[ A^{ij} D_{ij} u(x) = \tilde{A}^{ij} D_{ij} \tilde{u}(y) \] where \(\tilde{A}= P^T AP\). Why?

solution

Using Einstein summation convention. Let \(x=(x^1,\cdots,x^n)\),\(y=(y^1,\cdots,y^n)\).

\(\begin{aligned} D_{ij} u &= D_i(D_k \tilde{u}(y) y^k_j)\\ &=D_{lk} \tilde{u}(y) y^k_j y^l_i \end{aligned}\)

And from the question,

\(\begin{aligned} A^{ij} D_{ij} u &= A^{ij} y^k_j y^l_i D_{lk} \tilde{u}(y)\\ &= \tilde{A}^{lk} D_{lk} \tilde{u}(y) \end{aligned}\)

we has \(\tilde{A}^{lk} =A^{ij} y^k_j y^l_i\).

Notice that \(y=xP\), so \(y^k_j =P_{jk}\) and \(\tilde{A}^{lk} =A^{ij} P_{jk} P_{il}\). From it, we have \(\tilde{A}=P^T AP\).
